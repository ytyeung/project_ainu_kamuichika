## Isepo yaieyukar, “Sampaya terke”   
## 兎が自ら歌った謡「サンパヤ テレケ」 
## The Hare's Self Chant “Sampaya terke”   

Sanpaya Tereke

Leaping over two valleys, hopping over three valleys,
Playing along the way, I followed my older brother to mountains.
Days by days, I followed my older brother.
When we saw a crossbow trap set up by human [[1]](#f_1_4),
My older brother would destory it.
I was always laughing at this.

On this day, which I thought was just another day.
I never thought about it, unexpectedly,
My older brother was caught by the crossbow, crying out.
Shocked, I rushed to his side.
My brother said, weeping,
“My little brother, 
Immediately, run to the back of our village,
And shout loudly: Brother is caught in a crossbow —— Hu ohohoi.”  

I listened and replied,
“Yes, yes.” 
Then —— 

Leaping over two valleys, hopping over three valleys,
Playing along the way,
I arrived at the back of our village.

I finally remembered what my older brother had sent me to do.
I wanted to shout loudly,
The words I needed to say for my older brother,
I had completely forgotten. 
I stood there, frozen,
Trying to recall, 
But I could not.
Then, again,

Leaping over two valleys, hopping over three valleys,
Hopping backwards, leaping backwards,
Back to where my older brother was.
But no one was there.
Only left his blood everywhere.

(Here is another perspective of the story.)

Ketka woiwoi ketka, ketka woi ketka 

Days by days, I went to mountains.
Destroying crossbow traps set up by human.
Always finding it funny.

One day, at the usual place, I found a crossbow trap.
Beside, there was a small mugwort crossbow.
When I saw this, I thought,
“What is this? Is this really useful?”
Finding it laughable.

I touched it slightly, 
Wanted to escape immediately.
However, I never thought of,
I was caught by the small mugwort crossbow tightly.
I struggled to escape,
But the trap further squeezed.
I could do nothing but cried.
Someone rushed to my side.
My little brother when I looked.

Pleased, I told him to inform our village.
Long time had passed but no news.
Weeping, then,
A human shadow appeared beside me.
I saw a young man, as beautiful as a god.

He smiled and seized me,
Took me to somewhere.
I realized that,
It was a huge house,
Filled with divine treasures.

The young man lit a fire,
Placed a large pot over the flame,
Drew a hanging sword [[2]](#f_2_4),
Sliced my body, skin and bones, into small pieces,
Filled the pot.
He stretched out his head under the pot,
Stoking the fire.

I wanted to escape.
Therefore I watched for carelessness of the young man.
However, the young man always paid attention to me.

“When the pot boils, I will be cooked,
I will die a meaningless death, a bad death.”

Thinking, I watched for carelessness of the young man.
Finally, I transformed myself into a piece of meat,
Mixed my body with raising steam,
Climbed onto the edge of the pot,
Jumped down to the Left Seat,
Fled to outside immediately.
Crying, I fled to my house breathlessly,
Relieved.

Flashing back,
I thought that he was just a young man.
He was Okikirmui, as strong as a god.

I thought that the crossbow traps were set up by an originary human.
Therefore I played tricks every day and destroyed them.
Okikirmui was very angry, and wanted to kill me with a small mugwort crossbow.
I am not an originary god of low status.
If I were to suffer a meaningless death, a bad death,
My relatives would be confused and sad.

His mercy,
He did not chase after me when I fled.

Before, a hare was as large as a deer.
Because of my tricks,
I became as small as the piece of meat prepared by Okikirmui.

From now on, all our companions will be about this size.
From now on, hares, never play tricks.

The hare chief taught his children and died.

## Footnote  

### Chiri

<span id="f_1_4">[1] Amatsupo (crossbow): This refers to setting up a "trick bow". </span>

<span id="f_2_4">[2] Swords: These are different from the swords used in war. Normally, they are hung on the right side of the house, above the pile of treasures. They are not used in war, but they are used in ceremonies such as uniwente. </span>
