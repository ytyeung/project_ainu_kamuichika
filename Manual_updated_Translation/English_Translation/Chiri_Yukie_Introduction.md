## About Chiri Yukie

<div class="intro_box">
    <div class="intro_text">
        <p>
            <a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Yukie_Chiri" target="_blank">Chiri Yukie</a> (1903–1922) was an <a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ainu_people" target="_blank">Ainu</a> female from Hokkaido, born in what is now Noboribetsu. She became renowned for compiling and translating <em>Ainu Shinyōshū</em>, a collection of sacred Ainu epics and oral traditions. Raised in Asahikawa, she grew up surrounded by the living memory of Ainu storytelling, especially through her grandmother and aunt, who preserved the tradition of <em>kamuy yukar</em>.
        </p>
        <p>
            At the age of 15, Chiri met the Japanese linguist <a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ky%C5%8Dsuke_Kindaichi" target="_blank">Kindaichi Kyōsuke</a>, who encouraged her to transcribe and translate Ainu oral poems into Japanese. Drawing on her bilingual command of Ainu and Japanese, she recorded these stories in Romanized form so that they could be read and recited more widely. Her work helped preserve an important part of Ainu cultural heritage. Sadly, she died of heart failure in 1922 at the age of 19, just after completing the collection, but her writing remains one of the most important sources for understanding Ainu oral literature today.
        </p>
