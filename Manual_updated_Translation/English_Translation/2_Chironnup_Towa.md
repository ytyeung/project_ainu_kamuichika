## Chironnup yaieyukar, “Towa towa to” 
## 狐が自ら歌った謡「トワトワト」
## The Fox's Self Chant “Towa towa to”

Towa towa to
One day, I went to the seaside to gather food.
Stepping through the stones, Towa towa to.
Stepping through the wood chips, Towa towa to.
As I walked, I looked ahead and saw
A whale had washed ashore on the beach.

Men and women were in fine dresses,
Rejoicing and dancing over the gift of the sea. 
Cutting meat, carrying meat, back and forth.
Respectful chief offering thanks and prayers for the gift of the sea.
The beach was in black colour, filling with people sharpening knives.
Seeing this, I was delighted.
“I must hurry to get there, 
I want to receive even a little bit.”
I thought.
Then I shouted, “Ononno! Ononno!” 

Stepping through the stones, Towa towa to.
Stepping through the wood chips, Towa towa to.
I went closer and closer, but when I arrived,
Contrary to all expectations,
What I thought was a whale washed ashore,
Was just waste of dogs on the beach.
A large dump of waste with nasty smell.
I had mistaken for a whale.

I thought that people were rejoicing and dancing for the gift of the sea,
Cutting and carrying the meat.
But indeed they were crows,
Pecking and scattering the waste dump,
Flying around, flying left and right.

I was angry.
“You dim-eyed, worthless fool, Towa towa to.
You dull-eyed, wicked fellow, Towa towa to.
You smelly thing beneath the tail, Towa towa to.
You rotten thing beneath the tail, Towa towa to.
You resin coming out of your butt, Towa towa to.
You dirty water coming out of your butt, Towa towa to.
What a way to see things!”

Then again,
Stepping through the stones, Towa towa to.
Stepping through the wood chips, Towa towa to.
Walking along the seaside,
Looking forwards,
And saw a boat.
In the boat, 
Two people were exchanging condolences.

“Oh! What a sudden tragedy
Has caused their sadness?
Perhaps someone has capsized with the boat.
I must hurry,
Coming closer and hearing their sayings.”
Thinking this, I shouted loudly, 
“Hokokse!”

Stepping through the stones, Towa towa to.
Stepping through the wood chips, Towa towa to.

I ran quickly, and I found the fact.
I thought that it was a boat.
Indeed it was a rock on the beach.
I thought that they were people.
Indeed they were two large cormorants,
Stretching and shrinking their long necks,
As if they were exchanging condolences.

“You dim-eyed, worthless fool, Towa towa to.
You dull-eyed, wicked fellow, Towa towa to.
You smelly thing beneath the tail, Towa towa to.
You rotten thing beneath the tail, Towa towa to.
You resin coming out of your butt, Towa towa to.
You dirty water coming out of your butt, Towa towa to.
What a way to see things!”

Then again,
Stepping through the stones, Towa towa to.
Stepping through the wood chips, Towa towa to.
I went up the river with quick steps.
