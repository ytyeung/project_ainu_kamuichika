### Translation 1
```
(1) In the past, when a boy grew a little older, he would be given a small bow and arrow. The child would play by shooting at trees and birds, unknowingly improving his archery skills.
ak...... archery, shinot is a game, ponai is a small arrow.
(2) shiktumorke......gaze.
It is said that when you want to know a person's character, you can best understand it by looking at their eyes, and they will be scolded if they look around restlessly.
(3) achikara......means "dirty."
(4) It is said that birds and animals are shot down by people because they want the arrows made by people, so they take the arrows.
(5) kotankorkamui......god who owns a country or village.
In the mountains, there are nupurikorkamui......god who owns the mountain (bear) and nupuripakorkamui......god who owns the east of the mountain (wolf), and the owl is placed after the bear and wolf.
kotankorkamui is not as rough and hasty as the mountain god or the god of the east of the mountain. Therefore, he is usually calm and always closes his eyes, and it is said that he does not open his eyes unless something very serious happens.
(6) eharkiso......left seat.
(7) eshiso......right seat.
In the center of the house is a fireplace, and the side with the east-facing window is the upper seat. When viewed from the upper seat, the right side is eshiso and the left side is harkiso. Only men can sit in the upper seat. Guests who are of lower status than the head of the house will refrain from taking the upper seat. It is customary for the host and his wife to sit side by side in the right seat. The next seat after the right seat is the left seat, and the seat on the west side (towards the doorway) is the lowest seat.
(8) hayokpe helmet.
It is said that when birds or animals are in the mountains, they are invisible to human eyes, but each has a house like a human being, and they all live in the same form as humans, and when they come to human villages, they wear helmets. And the corpses of birds and animals are helmets, and the main body is invisible, but it is said to be between the ears of the corpse.
(9) otuipe......a guy with a cut-off butt.
Short tails like those of dogs are not highly valued.
Very trivial people are insulted as wenpe......bad guy, otuipe......guy with a cut-off tail.
(10) chikashnukar. When a god is very pleased with a human being, and unexpectedly bestows some great fortune on that person, that person rejoices, saying ikashnukar an.
(11) apehuchi......old woman of fire. The fire goddess is the most revered goddess in the house and is always an old woman. When mountain gods, sea gods, and other various gods come to the house as guests like this owl, this apehuchi takes the lead in entertaining the guests and talking to them. It is also acceptable to simply call her kamuihuchi (god old woman).
(12) neusar talking to each other.
Talking about various worldly things is also neusar. Usually, things like kamuiyukar (god song) and uwepeker (old tales) are called neusar.
(13) ashke a uk. ashke is finger, hand. a uk is to take. It means inviting people when there is a celebration.
(14) kakkokhau......the sound of the cuckoo bird.
The sound of the cuckoo bird is beautiful and clear to the ears, so people who speak clearly and understandably to everyone are said to be like cuckoo birds.
(15) chisekorkamui......god who owns a house.
The fire god is like the housewife, and the house god is like the master of the house. He is also called chisekorekashi......old man who owns a house.
(16) nusakorkamui......god who owns the gohei shelf, old woman.
The god of the gohei shelf is also always a woman. She may appear to humans in the event of an emergency, but it is said that she takes the form of a snake when she appears. Therefore, if a snake appears near the gohei shelf or near the east window, people say, "The old woman of the gohei shelf must have gone out on business," and they never kill the snake. It is said that if you kill it, you will be punished.
```

The translation accurately preserves the original meaning and is easy to understand. The language used is modern and simple.

#### Translation 2
```
(1) In the past, when a boy grew a little older, he would be given a small bow and arrow. The children would play by shooting at trees and birds, and unknowingly improve their archery skills.
ak... is archery, shinot is a game, ponai is a small arrow.
(2) shiktumorke... the look in one's eyes.
It is often said that if you want to know a person's true nature, the best way is to look into their eyes, so if someone's eyes are a little restless, they will be scolded.
(3) achikara... means "dirty."
(4) It is said that birds and beasts are shot down by humans because they want the arrows made by humans, so they go to retrieve them.
(5) kotankorkamui... a god who owns a country or village.
In the mountains, there are nupurikorkamui... a god who owns the mountain (bear), and nupuripakorkamui... a god who owns the east of the mountain (wolf), etc., and the owl is ranked next to the bear and wolf.
kotankorkamui is not as rough and flustered as the mountain god or the god of the east of the mountain. Therefore, they are usually calm and composed, and always keep their eyes closed, and only open them when something very serious happens.
(6) eharkiso... the left seat.
(7) eshiso... the right seat.
In the center of the house is a hearth, and the side with the east-facing window is the upper seat. Looking from the upper seat, the right side is eshiso and the left is harkiso. Only men can sit in the upper seat. Guests or people of lower status than the homeowner will refrain from sitting in the upper seat. The homeowner and his wife are always seated side by side in the right seat. The left seat is next to the right seat, and the seat on the west side (towards the doorway) is the lowest seat.
(8) hayokpe helmet.
It is said that when birds or beasts are in the mountains, they are invisible to human eyes, but they each have houses like humans, and they all live like humans. When they come to human villages, they wear helmets. The corpses of birds and beasts are helmets, and the main body is invisible, but it is between the ears of the corpse.
(9) otuipe... a guy with his butt cut off.
A short tail, like a dog's cut tail, is not highly valued.
Extremely useless people are insulted with wenpe... a bad guy, otuipe... a guy with his tail cut off.
(10) chikashnukar. When a god is very fond of a person, they will bestow great happiness on that person in a completely unexpected place, and that person will say ikashnukar an and rejoice.
(11) apehuchi... the old woman of fire. The fire god is the most revered god in the house and is always an old woman. When mountain gods, sea gods, and other various gods come to the house as guests like this owl, this apehuchi takes the lead in entertaining the guests and talking to them. It is also acceptable to simply call her kamuihuchi (god old woman).
(12) neusar talking to each other.
Talking to each other about various worldly matters is also called neusar. Usually, things like kamuiyukar (god songs) or uwepeker (old tales) are called neusar.
(13) ashke a uk. ashke is finger, hand. a uk is to take. It refers to inviting people when there is a celebration.
(14) kakkokhau... the call of the cuckoo.
The call of the cuckoo is clear and loud, so people use the cuckoo to describe someone who speaks clearly and makes themselves understood by everyone.
(15) chisekorkamui... a god who owns a house.
The fire god is like the housewife, and the house god is like the master of the house. Men are also called chisekorekashi... an old man who owns a house.
(16) nusakorkamui... a god who owns the altar, an old woman.
The god of the altar is also always female. In the event of an emergency, she may appear to humans, and it is said that she borrows the shape of a snake to appear. Therefore, if a snake appears near the altar or near the east-facing window, people will say, "The old woman of the altar must have gone out on business," and will never kill the snake. It is said that killing the snake will be punished.
```
